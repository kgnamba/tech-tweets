Draft Your Tweetorial

Write down the principle you want to explain.

It can be useful to write a question and then answer it. For example, 'What is a turing machine? A turing machine is...'. Don't worry about clarity or great writing, just get down your ideas.


Write down three concrete situations or examples related to your principle.

Try answering these prompts:

  1. Are there examples that illustrate your principle?
  2. What made you learn about this or why do you want to explain this?
  3. When might people (perhaps unknowingly) deal with this principle in their life?


Frame each situation as a question that would intrigue the reader.

The idea is that your explanation will answer this question.


Pick one question from above and up the stakes.

This will be your first tweet! Common techniques for increasing interest include:

  • relating it to a recent or emotional situation
  • correcting a common misconception
  • creating a sense of wonder or surprise
